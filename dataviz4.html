Data Visualization - The labor market, now and tomorrow

Trends in the labor market today and in the future are interesting in general. This section looks at the bigger trends for STEM-areas, and will not have a specific focus on girls and women.

Hard or easy for Employers to hire people with the right skills?

In this section we will look closer on the labor market and the STEM-sectors. Discovering from what College and University programs employers find it easy or hard to find newely grads from, and what needs they see in the future.

Hard to find newly graduates

77 percent of the employers say that they are having a hard time finding teachers grade 7-9 with a focus on mathematics, biology, physics and chemistry.

Employers also find it somewhat hard to find newly graduates with a degress from a Master of Science in Energy and Electronic Engineering and from a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering and Vehicle Engineering. They also astate that they have a far greater problem finding experiences employees in the same field.

Easy to find newly graduates

Following educational backgrounds employers find it easiest to find newly graduated from.

  • Architect (56 percent)
  • Bachelor of Engineering in Civil Engineering (50 percent)
  • Master of Science in Electronics, Computer Science and Automation (47 percent)

Balanced supply and demand of newly graduates

Newly graduates from programs focusing on biology, physics and chemistry seems to be in balance when it comes to supply and demand.
